Methodology / Description

This type of research draws on Helmer's durable-advantage lens, McKinsey's granular competitive-advantage approach, and practitioner venture thinking from a16z and Sequoia that focuses less on feature comparison and more on what actually drives customer choice, market power, and white space. I excluded generic checklist competitor matrices as the scoring backbone; the point here is to understand the structure of advantage, not just list vendors.

Dimensions

Category Structure

Whether the market is fragmented, concentrated, bundled, rapidly consolidating, or still unsettled.

Customer Choice Drivers

The attributes that actually determine customer selection and retention.

Competitor Advantage Quality

Whether leading competitors have real durable advantages or only temporary feature leads.

White-Space Quality

Whether meaningful unmet needs or underserved segments remain open.

Pace of Change

How quickly the landscape is shifting through technology, regulation, bundling, or buyer behavior.

Relative Positioning Risk

How exposed the focal company or idea is relative to current competitors.
